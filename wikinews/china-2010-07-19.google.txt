Chinese Government tries to contain oil spill in Yellow Sea
July 19, 2010
The Government of China sent an estimated 800 boats to try to contain the spill of crude oil occurred in two pipelines owned by the state-owned China National Petroleum Corporation, near the port city of Dalian, northeast of that country, and one of the most important.
Firefighters took about 15 hours to extinguish the blaze after an explosion in two pipelines, which caused the spill of 1,500 tonnes of crude oil in an area of 183 square kilometers of Bohai Sea. About 80 boats were carrying out cleaning and containment of energy.
Authorities estimated that the cleanup will take five days, while you are redirected million barrels of oil to other facilities. The accident occurred about 100 kilometers from the coast and shut down the port of Dalian. The industrial complex is one of the four largest in China, and is estimated to have reserves of 19 billion barrels.
